## Problem Statement

CPA candidates (22-year-olds at accounting firms) use Becker and Gleim for content but
have no social layer. They can't visually see their own study hours, feel isolated in
a long grind (avg 18 months, 70% fail at least once), and feel peer pressure from
colleagues without any way to channel it positively. No peer-to-peer accountability
tool exists for CPA candidates — the incumbents offer mentor-to-student accountability
(Kesler, Becker mentors), not student-to-student.

## Demand Evidence

No external demand evidence yet. Founder is his own target user — planning to take
the CPA exam himself and will use Ledger when he starts studying. The app is live at
https://ledgercpa.vercel.app but has no users.

Risk: product assumptions are based on anticipated behavior, not observed behavior.
First real test comes when founder starts studying.

## Status Quo

Candidates use Becker (~$1500-3000) or Gleim for structured content and question banks.
These are solo, content-heavy platforms. Study happens in isolation. Accountability, if
any, comes from mentors (expensive add-ons) or informal peer conversations. There is no
shared progress visibility between candidates at the same firm studying together.

## Target User & Chosen Approach

**User:** 22-year-old working at an accounting firm, actively pursuing CPA licensure.
Feels time-poor, can't visualize accumulated study hours, feels professional pressure
from peers who are also studying.

**Chosen approach (Companion Platform):** Keep the full feature set:
study hour tracking, social feed, friend/cohort leaderboard, question bank, AI coach,
flashcards, progress reports. Launch as a complete study companion alongside Becker/Gleim.

**Note from second opinion:** A leaner alternative would be study logging + social feed
only — test the core Strava thesis before building content features. Founder chose full
scope deliberately.

## Constraints

- Single-page HTML app (index.html, ~6800 lines) — no build step, deploys to Vercel via git push
- Firebase Auth (Google + email), Stripe billing already wired up
- Content in question bank must be CPA-accurate — this is a maintenance burden
- No backend beyond Firebase; AI coach features currently frontend-only (simulated)

## Premises

1. CPA candidates are motivated by seeing peers' study hours — visibility drives studying
2. The market gap is the peer-to-peer social layer — Becker/Gleim have content covered
3. 22-year-olds at accounting firms will post study activity publicly where coworkers can see it
   (Founder defended this against cross-model challenge)
4. Ledger works as a companion to Becker/Gleim, not a replacement

## Cross-Model Perspective

Claude subagent (cold read, no conversation context):

Steelman: "CPA candidates have a completion problem, not a content problem. Ledger adds
a commitment layer on top of existing content platforms, the same way Strava didn't
replace running shoes — it made people use them more."

Key insight from session: "'Feels pressure from peers at work.' This is the product.
The unit of acquisition isn't the individual candidate, it's the accounting firm cohort.
If one person at Deloitte uses it, their peers join because the alternative is being the
person who isn't visible."

Challenged premise: Public posting with coworkers. "CPA failure carries professional
stigma. Study hours at work are closer to salary data." Founder kept the premise,
arguing the social pressure outweighs the stigma risk.

48-hr prototype suggestion: Group-only study log + weekly leaderboard, skip everything
else — to test the core thesis before building further.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: Social Layer Only (not chosen)
Strip to study logging + social feed + leaderboard. Fastest path to testing the thesis.
Founder chose not to pursue — wants the full product story.

### Approach B: Companion Platform (chosen)
Full feature set: question bank, AI coach, flashcards, progress reports + social layer.
Launch as complete CPA study companion. Bets that content + social together is more
compelling and drives stronger retention than social alone.

### Approach C: Firm-Cohort B2B (not chosen)
Sell to accounting firms as onboarding/retention tool. Higher ceiling, much harder GTM.
Not the right stage for this yet.

## Recommended Approach

**Approach B — Companion Platform.**

Keep the full feature set. Polish the social feed and accountability core. Make the
study-logging action the primary CTA — everything else (question bank, AI coach,
flashcards) serves the goal of making people open Ledger daily.

The bet: content + social creates a stronger daily habit than social alone. When you
start studying yourself, you'll discover which content features you actually use and
which feel unnecessary. That's your real product roadmap.

Key things to get right before launch:
- Question bank content must be CPA-accurate (not placeholder)
- Study logging must be frictionless (one tap to start a session)
- Social feed must show real relative progress, not just cheering
- Onboarding should get a user to their first logged session in under 2 minutes

## Decisions Made

**Privacy model:** Default public feed (Strava-style). Add a "study privately" toggle
per session so users can log without broadcasting low-activity weeks. This preserves
the social thesis while reducing the stigma risk.

**Pricing model:** Freemium. Free tier includes study logging, social feed, and
leaderboard. Paid tier (~$10-15/mo) unlocks question bank, AI coach, and progress
reports. Stripe already wired — define free vs. paid feature gates before launch.

**AI coach:** Stub in v1 — show UI but disable or show "coming soon" behind the paid
gate. Do NOT expose API keys on the frontend. Real AI integration requires a Firebase
Cloud Function to proxy the API call. That is a v2 feature. For launch: paid tier
unlocks the AI coach section, but it displays a stub/waitlist state, not live AI.

**Question bank content:** Must be reviewed against AICPA blueprint before launch.
Gate all question bank content behind a "Beta — verify answers with your review course"
disclaimer until accuracy is confirmed. Flashcards and progress reports are deferred
from v1 spec — explicitly out of scope until question bank is validated.

**Firm-cohort virality (acquisition):** Current onboarding only supports inviting 1-3
friends. To actually capture firm cohorts, v1 needs: invite link that pre-populates a
"firm/group" field, and a simple cohort discovery screen (see who from your group is
already on Ledger). This is the mechanism behind the acquisition thesis — it must be
built for launch, not post-launch.

**Onboarding flow:** Sign up → set exam sections targeting → optional: invite 1-3
friends/coworkers → log first study session (section + hours). Target: under 2 min
from landing to first logged session.

**Architecture note:** Single-file HTML works for now. As features grow, the file
will become unmaintainable. Not blocking for launch, but plan to modularize after
first 100 users.

## Reviewer Concerns

Issues flagged by adversarial review that are intentionally deferred:
- Freemium paywall modal design (paywall UX — implement as standard upgrade modal)
- "Private" session semantics in feed (default: hide hours, show "logged a session")
- Single-file architecture maintainability (acknowledged; post-100-users concern)
- Stripe subscription flow details (trial period, cancellation — standard Stripe defaults for now)

## Success Criteria

- 10 users from founder's network join and log at least one session within 2 weeks of launch
- 3 of those 10 log sessions on 5+ distinct days in the first 2 weeks
- Average session logging takes under 60 seconds (frictionless)
- Zero users report incorrect CPA content before launch

## Distribution Plan

- Currently: GitHub push → Vercel auto-deploy (live at ledgercpa.vercel.app)
- Launch: share in CPA candidate communities (r/CPA, CPA Discord servers, study groups)
- Acquisition thesis: firm-cohort virality — one person invites their whole team
- No app store presence currently; web-only is fine for launch

## Dependencies

- CPA question bank content accuracy (blocking for real launch)
- Firebase free tier limits — need to monitor as users grow
- Stripe integration needs to be tested end-to-end before charging anyone

## The Assignment

**Start studying for the CPA exam this week.**

Not "plan to study" — pick a section, open Becker or Gleim, and log your first session
in Ledger. You're your own fastest validation path. After 2 weeks of real studying,
you'll know which features you actually use and which feel like noise. That's worth more
than any amount of upfront planning.

Then: share Ledger with 5 people you know who are also studying for the CPA. Not to
pitch them — just to see if they use it.
