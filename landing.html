The Reality

Most candidates fail because they lose track, not because they aren't smart.

The CPA exam has a ~50% first-attempt pass rate across all four sections (source: AICPA/NASBA published pass rate data). The candidates who fail aren't unprepared. They run out of time, lose momentum, or have no way to tell whether their current pace will get them through their exam window.

Ledger gives you real visibility: exactly how many hours you've put in, how many you need, and whether you're on track for each section, each week, every day.

Every feature in Ledger exists to keep you on pace, hold you accountable, and make sure you don't run out of time before you run out of sections.

Your whole firm studies together — and everyone can see your streak
Create a study club with classmates, coworkers, or firm-mates and watch each other's sessions appear in a live feed. The accountability effect is real. When your peers are logging 3-hour FAR sessions, it gets a lot harder to call it a night after 45 minutes. Join a public club or start a private one with your own group.
Core Feature
📊
Know exactly if you're on pace — or falling behind
Enter your exam date and weekly hour goal. Ledger calculates whether you'll hit your target and adjusts every time you log a session. If you're behind, it tells you now — not the week before your exam when it's too late to recover.
Core Feature
🏆
The leaderboard doesn't lie
See how your weekly hours stack up against every other Ledger user in your clubs and network. Watch your rank climb as your streak builds. When someone in your firm has already put in twice your hours this week, the motivation shows up fast.
Core Feature
📝
Scores, retakes, and your NTS countdown
Log every exam attempt — score, sit date, attempt number. Ledger auto-marks passed sections and when you don't pass, it shows your remaining NTS days and recalculates exactly how many hours you need before you sit again. Nothing slips through the cracks.
Core Feature
🤖
An AI coach that knows your entire study history
Ask Ledger anything — how does partnership basis work, which topics should I drill before Thursday, am I on pace before my NTS expires. The AI knows your hours, your sections, and your weak spots. You get answers tailored to where you actually are, not generic advice.
AI responses may contain errors — always verify against your official review course before your exam.
