## Design Context

### Users
Accounting students (primarily undergrad, early-career) who use Ledger daily as their primary CPA exam accountability tool. Context: between classes, evening grinds, late nights before exam windows. They need the app to feel motivating and social — it should make the study grind feel communal, not lonely.

### Brand Personality
**Motivating, social, warm** — like Strava crossed with a study group chat. Three words: **driven, communal, encouraging**. Not corporate. Not cold dashboard. Not just functional. Should make you want to open it and log hours.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light mode primary (students on phones/laptops in varied environments)
- **Primary palette**: Indigo (#4f46e5) is the right anchor — keep it
- **Exam colors are the semantic system**: FAR=red, AUD=blue, REG=green, BAR=amber, ISC=purple, TCP=teal — these should carry through everywhere (progress fills, chart bars, heatmap, session timer, activity cards)
- **Warmth**: Background should feel slightly warm/neutral, not cold blue-purple
- **Celebration moments**: Achievements, streaks, session completions deserve vivid color treatment

### Design Principles
1. **Exam colors own their sections** — every FAR reference is red-tinted, every REG reference is green-tinted, etc.
2. **Stats should feel like progress, not just numbers** — use color to signal good/behind/on-track
3. **Warmth over coolness** — prefer warm tinted neutrals over cold gray/purple backgrounds
4. **Celebrate the grind** — achievements and session recaps deserve color-rich moments
5. **Social signals need color weight** — leaderboard ranks, props, and streaks should feel alive
